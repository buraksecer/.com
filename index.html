What I Do

Database

To say information is to speak of databases. During the initial years of my profession, I extensively engaged with t-sql queries. Indeed, I delved into them so deeply that, even after I deleted my blog post about pivot tables, it remained listed at the top index on Google for a long time. I have worked with all sorts of databases - relational, document-based, or cache. Everyone is as fast as they are indexed.

Web

Now, we come to the mischievous child of my career. There's no doubt it has offered me numerous opportunities. The WEB, which typically backend developers despise but with which I have a nuanced relationship. I only write for the web in emergencies. I don't enjoy writing CSS. I prefer using what has already been written. My most recent web project involved rewriting a product detail page from scratch according to SEO optimization. It turned out to be a great project, with non-brand clicks increasing by up to 4 times in some countries and up to 100 times in others. Yes, countries :) It was a development of global scale for Delivery Hero (known as Yemeksepeti in TR).

API

There were topics we needed to discuss regarding the database, and I addressed these through the API services I developed. Without a doubt, a significant portion of my career has been devoted to backend development, which is both my favorite and most passionate area. I have participated in various architectures and projects. Depending on the needs, I've written microservices, event-based, and monolithic projects in changing infrastructures. If you haven't implemented response caching for your district-city-neighborhood table, I'd say just jot it down in your diary.

devOps

I've had the opportunity to gain experience exclusively in my own projects. Generally, I'm deeply interested in Linux, Docker, CI/CD, Jenkins, GitHub or GitLab actions, and currently, I'm closely engaged with AWS. Typically, companies have professionals who specialize in these areas. For me, it's both a hobby and a passion. These days, I'm savoring my coffee in the magical world of AWS

Mobile

Initially, I was interested in Android app development at university but lost interest later. Last year, I returned to mobile development with a startup project. I developed the Taxisst app using React Native. It seems like it's made by a team, but it's just us. We aim to be known in Turkey as the first to launch a shared taxi app.
